About This Course

A comprehensive BSc-level journey through Natural Language Processing, from classical statistical methods to state-of-the-art transformer architectures.

Course Overview

This Natural Language Processing course provides a rigorous introduction to the field, designed for undergraduate students in computer science, data science, and related disciplines. The curriculum bridges the gap between classical statistical language models and modern deep learning approaches.

Through 12 weeks of structured content, students progress from understanding basic n-gram models to implementing transformer-based architectures like BERT. Each week combines theoretical foundations with hands-on practical exercises.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Explain and implement statistical language models including n-grams with various smoothing techniques
  • Understand word embedding methods (Word2Vec, GloVe) and their mathematical foundations
  • Design and train recurrent neural networks (RNNs) and LSTMs for sequence modeling
  • Implement attention mechanisms and understand the transformer architecture
  • Fine-tune pre-trained language models (BERT, GPT) for downstream NLP tasks
  • Apply efficient training techniques including LoRA, quantization, and knowledge distillation
  • Evaluate NLP systems using appropriate metrics and identify potential biases
  • Make informed decisions about model selection based on scaling laws and compute budgets

Course Structure

The course is organized into four progressive parts:

Part 1: Foundations (Weeks 1-3)

Statistical language models, word embeddings, and recurrent neural networks. Building intuition through hands-on experiments with n-grams, Word2Vec, and LSTM architectures.

Part 2: Core Architectures (Weeks 4-6)

Sequence-to-sequence models, attention mechanisms, and the transformer architecture. Deep dive into BERT and pre-training paradigms.

Part 3: Advanced Topics (Weeks 7-9)

Scaling laws, tokenization strategies (BPE, WordPiece), and text generation methods including beam search and nucleus sampling.

Part 4: Applications (Weeks 10-12)

Parameter-efficient fine-tuning with LoRA, model compression techniques, and responsible AI development including bias detection and mitigation.

Technology Stack

This course uses industry-standard tools and frameworks:

Python 3.8+ PyTorch Hugging Face Transformers Jupyter Notebooks NumPy Matplotlib scikit-learn NLTK

Prerequisites

Students should have:

  • Proficiency in Python programming
  • Understanding of basic linear algebra (vectors, matrices, operations)
  • Familiarity with probability and statistics fundamentals
  • Introduction to machine learning concepts (supervised learning, gradient descent)

For students needing a refresher, the course includes optional primer modules on neural networks and LSTM fundamentals.
